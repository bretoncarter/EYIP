Our Curriculum

Our curriculum is designed to be a semester long after school course for high school students. Each student assumes the role of a Senator and completes coursework for that role. Each class is centered around a specific theme that allows students to simultaneously learn about politics while gaining core hands-on skills. The course is divided into nine lectures culminating in an open house presentation where students can demonstrate what they have learned and show the materials they have created over the course of the semester. Our curriculum provides for growth and teamwork while honestly discussing the various and complicated aspects of running a campaign.

Overview

Week Subject Content
1 Launch and Resaerch Introduction to the course, EYIP and instructors. Cover basic research methods.
2 Platform Production and Speech Writing Construct a campaign platform, begin doing research and write speech.
3 Media Consider various and different channels of media and launch your own media campaign.
4 Campaign Finance Discuss the finances of a campaign and create a budget sheet for your own.
5 Public Speaking Learn basic public speaking skills and practice your own speech with fellow classmates.
6 Writing Bills and Making Laws Review how bills become laws, the importance of language and see how legislation affects you.
7 The Presidency Understand the basic powers of the President, and learn about current issues concerning the President. Create and debate a related argument.
8 The Supreme Court See how the Supreme Court is structured, analyze the Constitution and discuss how it affects us today.
9 The Presidency Understand the responsibilities of the President and how his position affects everyday people.
10 Final Presentation Compile all work for the semester to be able to present to teachers, parents and a few special guests.
